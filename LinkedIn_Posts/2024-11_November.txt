#DAY92 #What is OAuth and Why Does it Matter? Ever used the "Sign in with Google" or "Login with Facebook" button? That's OAuth in action! #OAuth (Open Authorization) is a security framework that allows apps to access your information without needing your password. Think of it like giving a friend a spare key to just your living room, not your whole house. Here's how it works in three easy steps: You Approve Access: When an app (like a fitness app) asks to access your Google account, OAuth steps in. You're redirected to Google to log in, but you never give the app your Google password. Google Issues a Key (Token): After you approve, Google gives a unique "access token" to the app. This token lets the app access ONLY the data you allowed, like your calendar or contacts. Limited Access, Full Security: Now, the app can fetch data (like your schedule) without storing your credentials. #Why Use OAuth? Keeps your password private Limits access to only what you approve Provides temporary access, revokable anytime #OAuth is the technology behind seamless logins, secure data sharing, and safe access in the apps we use every day. Next time you log in with Google or Facebook, you can thank OAuth for keeping things both simple and secure!

As a CNCF project, it unifies observability data, making it easier for developers to monitor and troubleshoot complex architectures. #Day83 What is Splunk? Splunk is a platform for searching, monitoring, and analyzing big data from diverse sources like servers, apps, and network devices. It provides real-time insights that help organizations troubleshoot, secure, and optimize operations. Why Use Splunk? • Data Analysis & Visualization: Detailed dashboards make it easy to analyze data in real-time, spot patterns, and identify anomalies. • Centralized Log Management: All logs are stored in one place, enhancing visibility across systems. • Security & Compliance: Detects threats and assists in incident investigations and regulatory compliance. • Operational Efficiency: Automates incident response workflows, minimizing downtime. • Scalability: Manages large data volumes as organizations grow. How Splunk Works 1. Data Collection: Collects data from various sources, handling all types of formats. 2. Indexing & Parsing: Transforms data into events for quick retrieval, making it searchable. 3. Search & Query: Users leverage Splunk's Search Processing Language (SPL) to analyze data and build visualizations. 4. Alerting & Reporting: Set up real-time alerts and schedule reports for proactive monitoring. 5. Machine Learning: ML tools predict trends, detect anomalies, and enhance data correlation for security and planning.
