ID:7397642482081099776 New Edition is live Kubernetes is really important for AI tools, helping them manage their tricky tasks. Many AI projects don't succeed because of problems with the tech setup they run on, not usually because the AI itself is flawed. Kubernetes handles simple AI tools well. But for really big projects that go live, it needs to improve. For AI tools to work best on Kubernetes, we need better ways to handle who can access what (identity), use graphics cards (GPU), watch how things are running (observability), and keep everything safe (security).

===POST_82=== DATE:2025-11-21
ID:7397480496735252480 I had the chance to moderate our #KubeCon2025 panel — thank you Damian Igbe, PhD for organizing and Improving for hosting such a great community session. A huge thank you to our panelists: Danny Williams, Napoleon Musonera, Jeff Holsinger, and Jeremy Hallock for sharing insights. Here are the 10 takeaways the audience walked away with: 1 AI is moving fast, but it needs guardrails. Kubernetes can run AI agents, but only with safe protocols like A2A and MCP. 2 Image security is the biggest real risk. Too many teams still pull public images without checking anything. 3 Dashboards aren't the answer. The real shift is toward AI-assisted SRE that reduces noise and speeds up troubleshooting. 4 Not everything should be a microservice. One VM can sometimes be safer, simpler, and easier to manage. 5 Supply chain security needs more attention. Version tags can be misleading. Signed images and SHAs matter more than ever. 6 AI will split engineering roles. You'll either build AI or learn to use it extremely well — the "middle" work is shrinking. 7 Kubernetes will get both simpler and more complex. Simpler for people using AI tools, more complex for people building AI systems. 8 Most learning at KubeCon came from real conversations. Hallways, maintainers, demo theaters — not the slides. 9 Tools worth watching: NewVector, Chainguard Images, Kubescape, Feast, Kubeflow, Argo, OTel improvements. 10 Final reminder from the panel: Secure early. Learn deeply. Talk to people. Community teaches more than keynotes. Grateful to all our panelists and everyone who joined, asked questions, and stayed to network.
