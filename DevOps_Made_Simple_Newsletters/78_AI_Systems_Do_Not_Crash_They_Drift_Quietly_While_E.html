AI Systems Do Not Crash: They Drift Quietly While Everything Looks Healthy

View on LinkedIn

✍️ Poojitha A S | SRE | AI Observability Series

Most software fails loudly.

Errors spike. Latency explodes. Alerts fire.

You know something is wrong.

AI systems behave differently.

They degrade quietly while dashboards stay green. CPU normal. Latency normal. HTTP 200 across the board.

The system appears healthy.

But the answers are wrong.

And users trust them.

The Scale of the Problem

In 2024 AI hallucinations cost businesses 67.4 billion dollars.

In 2025 95 percent of enterprise AI initiatives never reached production. MIT Media Lab confirmed it.

47 percent of enterprise AI users made at least one major decision based on hallucinated output.

A lawyer filed fabricated case citations. A chatbot response erased 100 billion dollars in shareholder value. A health insurance algorithm denied care at one claim per second with a 90 percent error rate on appeals.

None of these triggered an infrastructure alert.

Why Traditional Monitoring Fails

Most teams monitor AI the same way they monitor traditional software. CPU. Memory. Latency. Error rate.

Those signals work for deterministic systems. Same input. Same output.

AI does not work that way.

Large language models are non deterministic. A model can have 99.9 percent uptime while producing completely wrong outputs.

A system that is up but wrong is worse than a system that is down. When it is down people wait. When it is wrong people trust it.

Five Failure Modes Monitoring Misses

1 Silent Quality Degradation. Model responds fast. Answers are wrong. Dashboards stay green. Users leave.

2 Prompt Drift. Someone edits the system prompt in a database. No deploy. No config change tracked. Behavior changes silently.

3 Stale Embeddings. Embedding pipeline fails. Vector database serves outdated content. Model is not hallucinating. Your data pipeline is.

4 Token Cost Explosions. Agent retries at 2 AM. Each retry includes growing history. Monthly budget gone by sunrise. CPU looks fine. This is a wallet problem.

5 Behavioral Anomalies. The model refuses what it used to answer. Or answers what it used to block. Nothing crashed. Behavior changed.

The AI Golden Signals

Infrastructure has four golden signals. AI needs its own.

1 Response Quality. Is the answer correct? Not fast. Not available. Correct.

2 Token Cost. Track by model, application, environment. This is your AI electricity bill.

3 Drift. Data drift. Model drift. Embedding drift. Small daily changes until quality collapses.

4 Retrieval Relevance. Did retrieved documents actually help? Ten irrelevant results is worse than zero.

5 Guardrail Health. How often do guardrails fire? Sudden spikes mean misuse, attacks, or misconfiguration.

Anomaly Detection: The Missing Layer

Traditional anomaly detection watches for spikes. CPU above 90 percent. Disk at 95.

AI anomaly detection watches for changes in meaning.

Good platforms build baselines for response quality, token patterns, and semantic output. When something deviates, they correlate signals across layers.

The Tools That Matter

Enterprise: Dynatrace, Datadog, New Relic. Best if you already run them.

AI Native: Arize Phoenix, LangWatch, Braintrust. Built specifically for AI.

Open Standard: OpenTelemetry. Instrument once. Send anywhere. No vendor lock in.

The AI Quality Budget

SRE teams use error budgets. AI teams need quality budgets.

Target 95 percent accuracy. Allow 5 percent failure. That is your experimentation budget.

Week 1 → New prompt. Quality 96 percent. Budget healthy.

Week 2 → Model update. Quality 93 percent. Budget dropping.

Week 3 → Rollback. Quality 95 percent. Stabilized.

Week 4 → Pipeline fails. Quality 88 percent. Budget gone.

Result: Feature freeze. Team focuses on data pipeline health.

Where To Start This Week

Day 1. Add OpenTelemetry traces to every LLM call and retrieval step.

Day 2. Run automated quality evaluation on a sample of production responses.

Day 3. Build a token usage dashboard. Alert on any 3x spike.

Day 4. Check embedding and vector database freshness.

Day 5. Baseline quality, cost, guardrails, and retrieval relevance.

These become your AI golden signals.

Start there.

You cannot eliminate AI hallucinations.

But you can detect them in minutes instead of days.

Traditional observability tells you the system is running.

AI observability tells you the system is correct.
