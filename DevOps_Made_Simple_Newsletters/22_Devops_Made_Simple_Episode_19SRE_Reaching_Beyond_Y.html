POOJITHA A S | Jul 28 Welcome to another edition of DevOps Made Simple : where we take big complex topics and make them feel like a conversation over coffee

Hey folks 👋

Let me take you back to a moment that changed the way I think about SRE

We had just launched a platform update. Everything on our side looked green But our internal users were frustrated. Complaining that APIs were timing out We checked the logs. Nothing. Monitoring looked clean So we told them "Everything's fine on our end"

But it didn't matter They were experiencing pain. And pain speaks louder than dashboards

That day I learned something important

Your monitoring doesn't define your reliability. Your users do

Here's what I've learned since then from practicing SRE beyond our team

💡 Five Powerful Lessons That Changed How I See SRE

1. Reliability is everything If your system isn't reliable people stop using it No matter how fast or advanced it is if it breaks it loses trust

2. Your users decide what's broken You might say "Our alerts didn't fire" They say "Your API ruined our night" And that's what they remember when choosing who to trust next

3. If you run a platform you share responsibility Maybe your API has 99.999 percent uptime But their app built on your platform runs at 99 percent Guess who they'll blame You

4. Every system eventually becomes a platform Even without APIs people find ways to integrate If you don't support it properly you lose control of the experience

5. Frustrated users lead to lost time Every complaint every workaround takes energy away from building And yes even internal teams are your users Supporting them is part of the job

🧠 So What Can You Do

If you want fewer outages fewer complaints and more trust Start practicing SRE with your customers

Here's how

🔄 Five Steps to Practicing SRE With Your Customers

1. Speak in SLOs Help your customers define SLOs If they don't they'll make one up and you won't hear about it until you break it Using the same language helps everyone stay aligned

2. Build shared dashboards Review their alerts and dashboards Work together to track the right things This avoids confusion and makes collaboration easier

3. Collect data and adjust expectations Most teams think they're getting five nines Truth is they're closer to 99.5 percent And if their users aren't complaining maybe that's good enough

4. Review their architecture Help them find weak spots Look for single points of failure manual rollbacks and risky deployments Fixing just a few key things can improve reliability fast

5. Practice issues together Simulate outages Run joint exercises and reviews This builds muscle memory and trust when things go wrong.

🏄 Pick Your Customer Focus

You can't do this with everyone So choose based on what matters

  • Focus on your top revenue customers

  • Or choose the most used features

  • Or sample different types of workloads

Pick one approach and stick to it

🧵 TLDR

  • Reliability is shared

  • SLOs are your shared language

  • Monitoring is your shared lens

  • Trust is your shared goal

Closing Thought 💬

Tools are great But SRE is just as much about the people using your system as it is about the system itself

Reliability isn't only about uptime. It's about how much your users trust you

Keep listening Keep improving Keep reaching beyond your walls
