Why most migrations fail and how to do it right

✍️ By Poojitha A S DevOps | SRE | Cloud | Kubernetes Adapted from real-world migration failures and successes

"Most cloud migrations don't fail because of technology. They fail because teams migrate infrastructure without migrating thinking."

Cloud migration is often sold as simple.

Reality? It's one of the highest-risk engineering transformations a company can attempt.

In this edition, I'm breaking down the 10 most common on-prem → cloud migration problems, why they actually happen, and what experienced SRE teams do differently.

No vendor bias. No theory. Only what works in the field.

Problem #1: Unexpected Cost Overruns

Category: Financial | Severity: High

What teams experience Cloud bills explode — sometimes 2–4× higher than original estimates.

Why this happens

  • Lift-and-shift with on-prem sizing

  • No cost ownership per team

  • Always-on resources

  • Data egress ignored

What actually works

  • Cost ownership per service/team

  • Budgets + alerts from Day 1

  • Autoscaling by default

  • Regular right-sizing reviews

SRE reality check If no one owns the bill, everyone overspends.

Problem #2: Data Transfer Bottlenecks

Category: Performance | Severity: High

What teams experience Data migration takes weeks or months and impacts production.

Why this happens

  • Underestimating data volume

  • Migrating everything at once

  • Production systems competing for bandwidth

What actually works

  • Tiered migration (hot vs cold data)

  • Offline or staged transfer strategies

  • Compress, dedupe, and validate early

SRE insight Data is usually the hardest part, not compute.

Problem #3: Security & Compliance Gaps

Category: Security | Severity: High

What teams experience Security controls don't translate cleanly to cloud.

Why this happens

  • Assuming cloud provider handles everything

  • Manual security processes

  • IAM designed after migration, not before

What actually works

  • Shared responsibility model clarity

  • Identity-first architecture

  • Policy as code (not checklists)

  • Continuous security monitoring

Hard truth Cloud doesn't make you secure. Good architecture does.

Problem #4: Application Compatibility Issues

Category: Technical | Severity: High

What teams experience Legacy apps fail or behave unpredictably in cloud.

Why this happens

  • Tight coupling to hardware

  • Stateful designs

  • Assumptions about static infrastructure

What actually works

  • Assess before migrating

  • Re-platform where possible

  • Refactor only when it brings real value

SRE lesson Not everything should be containerized on Day 1.

🏠 Problem #5: Skills Gap & Training Needs

Category: Organizational | Severity: Medium

What teams experience Bad architecture decisions and operational pain.

Why this happens

  • Same on-prem mindset in cloud

  • No hands-on enablement

  • Tool adoption without understanding

What actually works

  • Internal enablement programs

  • Platform guardrails

  • Clear patterns and golden paths

Leadership takeaway Training is cheaper than outages.

Problem #6: Migration Downtime & Cutover Risks

Category: Operations | Severity: High

What teams experience Failed cutovers, long outages, rollback chaos.

Why this happens

  • Big-bang migrations

  • No rehearsal

  • Poor communication

What actually works

  • Phased migrations

  • Blue/green or parallel runs

  • Tested rollback plans

SRE mantra If rollback isn't tested, it doesn't exist.

Problem #7: Vendor Lock-In Concerns

Category: Strategic | Severity: Medium

What teams experience Fear of being stuck with one provider.

Why this happens

  • Heavy use of proprietary services

  • No abstraction strategy

What actually works

  • Intentional trade-offs

  • Open standards where it matters

  • Portability for core systems

Reality check Lock-in isn't bad — unplanned lock-in is.

Problem #8: Performance & Latency Issues

Category: Performance | Severity: Medium

What teams experience Apps slower than on-prem.

Why this happens

  • Network assumptions break

  • Shared infrastructure realities

  • Poor region placement

What actually works

  • Measure before tuning

  • Co-locate dependent services

  • Design for failure, not perfection

Problem #9: Disaster Recovery & Backup Gaps

Category: Operations | Severity: High

What teams experience False sense of safety until an incident hits.

Why this happens

  • Assuming cloud backups are automatic

  • No DR testing

  • Region ≠ backup

What actually works

  • Explicit backup strategy

  • Tested restore procedures

  • DR as code

SRE truth Backups are useless if restore isn't tested.

Problem #10: Governance & Shadow IT

Category: Organizational | Severity: Medium

What teams experience Resource sprawl and compliance issues.

Why this happens

  • Unlimited self-service

  • No guardrails

  • No visibility

What actually works

  • Policy-driven provisioning

  • Standardized platforms

  • Clear ownership and accountability

TL;DR

  • Cloud migration is organizational change, not just infra change

  • Most failures come from assumptions, not tools

  • SRE thinking reliability, ownership, and feedback loops makes the difference

  • Migrate deliberately, not desperately
