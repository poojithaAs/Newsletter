"In microservices, the real problem isn't failure — it's how fast you find the fix."

When issues hit production, speed and precision matter most. This guide gives you essential commands and quick-response steps that experienced DevOps and SRE engineers rely on during critical moments.

Quick Start: For Beginners

These three commands are your starting point when something feels off.

1. Check Pod Status

Purpose: See all running pods, their status (like CrashLoopBackOff), and which nodes they're on. 
Time Saved: Understand the problem scope in under 30 seconds.
2. Get Pod Logs
Purpose: Read recent logs to find application errors or startup problems.
 Time Saved: Identify root causes in 1–2 minutes.
3. Describe a Pod
Purpose: View detailed information — events, container health, resource usage, and node details. 
Time Saved: Spot configuration or scheduling issues in 2–3 minutes. Emergency Troubleshooting Steps
When a service goes down, follow this flow systematically:
  1. Initial Alert — Check dashboards or monitoring alerts to confirm the incident.
  2. Check Pods — kubectl get pods -o wide; look for CrashLoopBackOff, Pending, or Error.
  3. Review Logs — kubectl logs <pod-name>; add --previous for restarted pods.
  4. Inspect Pod Details — kubectl describe pod <pod-name> for image, storage, or scheduling issues.
  5. Network Check — curl from within a pod; verify kubectl get endpoints.
  6. Isolate & Fix — Roll back or apply the fix and keep the team informed.

Network & Connection Problems

Purpose: Verify service-to-pod mapping. 
Time Saved: Confirm links in 30 seconds.
Solve Network Policy Problems
Purpose: Detect traffic blocked by NetworkPolicies. Time Saved: Identify restrictions in 1 minute.
Setup & Data Flow Issues
Trace Requests in Logs
Purpose: Follow a request manually when observability lags. Time Saved: Trace paths in 3–5 minutes.
Detect Configuration Changes
Purpose: Compare ConfigMaps across environments to catch drift. Time Saved: Spot mismatches in 2–3 minutes.
Advanced Debugging
Check Crashed Pod Logs
Purpose: Retrieve logs from before a crash. Time Saved: Understand restarts in ~2 minutes.
Debug Node Issues
Purpose: Inspect node-level CPU/memory without SSH. Time Saved: Assess node health in ~5 minutes.
