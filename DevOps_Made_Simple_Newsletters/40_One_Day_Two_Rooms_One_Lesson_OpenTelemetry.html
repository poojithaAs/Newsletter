One Day, Two Rooms, One Lesson: OpenTelemetry

View on LinkedIn

✍️ By Poojitha A S | #DevOps #SRE #Kubernetes #OpenTelemetry

"Observability isn't just about things being 'up and running'. It's about really understanding how our systems affect users and the business."

Last week was a blur of activity. In the Afternoon, I was at an Open Space discussion during Devopsdays Dallas , hearing honest, unscripted stories about OpenTelemetry (OTEL). By the evening, I was at the Kubernetes & Cloud Native Dallas Meetup, where showed us OTEL in action with a live demo.

Two very different places — one for casual chats, the other for a deep dive. But the main message was clear: OTEL is now a must-have. The real trick is how to use it wisely.

Community Voices (DevOpsDays Open Space)

🔥 Excitement

Freedom from lock-in is real: 👉 "If prices triple, I just flip a switch to a new tool."

That's the power of OTEL. It's vendor-agnostic, community-driven, and quickly becoming the official CNCF standard.

⚠️ Cost Traps to Avoid

A practitioner shared: "We burned 11 months of budget in 8 hours." The reason? Collecting the wrong data.

👉 Lesson: If you don't control cost at the collector, OTEL quickly becomes "open wallet telemetry."

🏗️ Architects' Playbook

Think of collectors as little helpers next to your apps, grabbing logs, metrics, and traces. Gateways? They're the managers — deciding where data goes, filtering it, and keeping things efficient and secure.

🔐 Security Lens

Multiple collectors and gateways = safety switches. They reduce single points of failure, isolate sensitive data, and make governance easier.

🛠️ Ops & SRE Reality Check

"Don't bet your sleep on it working perfectly just yet." Start small. Test often. Build confidence before you scale.

🌍 How Industry Leaders Use OTEL

🚗 Tesla – Custom OTEL + Prometheus + Clickhouse stack. 👉 Every millisecond and penny matters, so they fine-tune for speed, scale, and cost efficiency.

⚙️ Toyota – Uses Backstage developer portals to enforce strict OTEL package usage. 👉 Ensures consistency, prevents costly mistakes, and tells a reliable data story across global teams.

🚀 Alloy – Runs a forked OTEL collector pre-baked for their unique environment. 👉 Standards adapted to their needs = seamless integration and scale.

✨ Big Picture: OTEL isn't "set it and forget it." It's discipline, rules, and governance. Done right, it's freedom + resilience.

📦Expert Insights (Kubernetes amp; Cloud Native Meetup)

Rajeev Govindan shared similar ideas but from a big-business viewpoint:

  • 🌐 Why OTEL matters: It's becoming the go-to way to collect all your data (logs, metrics, traces).

  • 🖥️ The Collector is a game-changer: It takes in data, processes it (filters, samples, batches), and then sends it out. It's your main control point for managing costs and data quality.

  • 🛠️ How to start using it: Begin by letting it automatically set itself up, try it on less important apps first, then slowly roll it out more widely.

  • 🔄 Moving to new systems: Send your data to both the old system and the new one at the same time. This lets you compare them and switch over safely.

  • 💸 Saving money: Filter out unnecessary data early, smartly pick which data to keep, and make sure your data labels are clean and consistent.

  • 💡 Live demo: He showed how Coralogix and OTEL work together to create reliable data pipelines, flexible ways to send data out, and faster problem-solving.

👉 Key Lesson: Once you standardize how you collect data with OTEL, choosing a vendor becomes a matter of picking the best dashboard, not being stuck with one company.

Observability isn't just about hooking things up — it's about freedom to choose, setting limits, and making a real business impact.
