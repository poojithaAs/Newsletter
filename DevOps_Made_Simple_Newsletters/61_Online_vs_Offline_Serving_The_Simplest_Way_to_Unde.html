Online vs Offline Serving: The Simplest Way to Understand MLOps

View on LinkedIn

✍️ By Poojitha A S

Most ML confusion starts with this one idea. If the model answers right now, it's online. If it answers later, it's offline.

Let's break both in the simplest way.

🔙 Online Serving (Real-Time Predictions)

Online serving means the model gives predictions instantly as soon as someone requests it.

Think of it like:

  • Food delivery apps showing ETA immediately

  • Netflix suggesting movies the moment you open the app

  • Credit card companies detecting fraud while you swipe

How it works (very simple):

  1. The model is deployed behind an API

  2. A user/app sends a request

  3. The model replies in milliseconds

Best for:

Personalization, fraud detection, chatbots, anything needing fast response

🔙 Offline Serving (Batch Predictions)

Offline serving means predictions are made ahead of time, usually on a schedule.

Think of it like:

  • Spotify creating your daily playlist overnight

  • Monthly churn reports for all users

  • Weekly financial risk analysis

How it works (very simple):

  1. Data is collected over hours/days

  2. A batch job runs (Airflow, Databricks, Spark)

  3. Predictions are stored in a table for later use

Best for:

Large datasets, reports, dashboards, low-cost prediction jobs

Online = prediction now.

Offline = prediction prepared earlier.
