Cloud Cost Optimization That Actually Works

View on LinkedIn

Reality check: Most engineering teams waste 30–40% of their cloud budget on idle resources, oversized instances, and forgotten environments.

The good news? These are the easiest savings to capture often without changing architecture.

Why Cloud Bills Spiral Out of Control

Cloud spending is now the #2 operational expense for many tech companies. Yet most teams have little to no visibility into what they are paying for and why.

Common cost drivers

  • Zombie resources Dev environments running 24/7, unused load balancers, old snapshots

  • Over-provisioned instances Running large instance types "just in case"

  • No commitment strategy Paying on-demand prices for predictable workloads

  • No cost ownership Teams don't know which service or feature drives spend

FinOps (What It Really Means)

FinOps is not a tool.

It is a cultural practice where engineering, finance, and business teams collaborate to make data-driven spending decisions that maximize business value.

When costs are visible during design not after billing engineering decisions improve.

The 5 Quick Wins (Week 1)

1. Kill Zombie Resources

Use Cost Explorer and CloudWatch to identify near-zero utilization.

Typical savings: 15–20% of total spend
2. Right-Size Over-Provisioned Instances
AWS Compute Optimizer provides free, actionable recommendations.
Most teams discover 30–40% of instances are oversized.
Example: 10 × r5.2xlarge → r5.xlarge Savings: ~$42,000/year
3. Auto-Shutdown Non-Production Environments
Dev and staging environments don't need to run nights and weekends.
  • Lambda-based scheduler
  • Weekdays, business hours only
Result: ~65% savings on non-prod compute
4. Delete Old EBS Snapshots
Snapshots older than 30 days are rarely needed.
  • Apply lifecycle policies
  • Auto-delete unused backups
Easy win: Storage costs drop immediately.
5. Use Spot Instances for Batch Workloads
Spot instances are 70–90% cheaper than on-demand.
Best for:
  • CI/CD pipelines
  • Data processing
  • ML training
  • Non-critical batch jobs
Reserved Instance Strategy (Weeks 2–3)
Reserved Instances and Savings Plans offer 40–75% discounts when used correctly.
How to Calculate RI Coverage
Look at the last 90 days of usage.
Prioritize:
  • Instances running 24/7
  • Databases with steady load
  • NAT gateways and load balancers
Avoid committing on:
  • Dev/test
  • Spiky or experimental workloads

Building a FinOps Culture
Cost optimization fails without ownership.
1. Make Costs Visible
  • Tag resources by team, service, environment
  • Share weekly cost dashboards
2. Assign Cost Ownership
  • Each team owns a monthly cloud budget
  • Track actuals during sprint planning
3. Bring Cost Awareness Into PRs
When engineers see costs during development, architecture improves naturally.
Essential FinOps Tools
