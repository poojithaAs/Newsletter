#DAY137 #KubernetesNetworkingCommands Basic Pod and Node Inspection kubectl get pods -o wide: View pods with their node assignments and IP addresses. kubectl describe pod <pod_name>: Detailed information about a specific pod&#x27;s configuration and networking. kubectl get nodes: View all nodes in the cluster and their statuses. kubectl describe nodes <node_name>: Inspect the configuration, routes, and taints of a specific node. Pod Communication and Connectivity kubectl exec -it <pod_name> -- /bin/sh: Access a pod for testing connectivity or debugging. curl <pod_ip>:<port>: Test direct connectivity to a pod. kubectl get svc: List all services and their ClusterIP or NodePort details. kubectl describe svc <service_name>: Get detailed information about a service and its backend pods. Services and DNS kubectl get services: View all services in the cluster, including ClusterIP and NodePort types. curl http://<service_name>.<namespace>.svc.cluster.local: Access a service via its DNS name. kubectl logs <pod_name> -c coredns: Inspect logs of CoreDNS for troubleshooting DNS issues. Routing and IP Management ip route: Check routing rules on a node. kubectl get endpoints <service_name>: View the endpoint IPs associated with a service. kubectl get ep: Shortcut for listing all endpoints in the cluster. kubectl get pods --all-namespaces -o wide: Inspect IPs of pods across namespaces for troubleshooting.
