#DAY81 𝗧𝗲𝗿𝗿𝗮𝗳𝗼𝗿𝗺 𝘃𝘀. 𝗢𝗽𝗲𝗻𝗧𝗼𝗳𝘂: 𝗪𝗵𝗶𝗰𝗵 𝗦𝗵𝗼𝘂𝗹𝗱 𝗬𝗼𝘂 𝗖𝗵𝗼𝗼𝘀𝗲? 𝗕𝗮𝗰𝗸𝗴𝗿𝗼𝘂𝗻𝗱 & 𝗛𝗶𝘀𝘁𝗼𝗿𝘆 𝗧𝗲𝗿𝗿𝗮𝗳𝗼𝗿𝗺: Introduced by HashiCorp in 2014, Terraform became a favorite tool for Infrastructure-as-Code (IaC). However, in 2023, HashiCorp changed its license to a Business Source License (𝗕𝗦𝗟), limiting some usage rights. 𝗢𝗽𝗲𝗻𝗧𝗼𝗳𝘂: OpenTofu (formerly OpenTF) was created in response to Terraform&#x27;s licensing change.

It&#x27;s a community-driven, open-source alternative, maintaining full open-source freedom. 𝗕𝗲𝗻𝗲𝗳𝗶𝘁𝘀 & 𝗨𝘀𝗲 𝗖𝗮𝘀𝗲𝘀 𝗧𝗲𝗿𝗿𝗮𝗳𝗼𝗿𝗺: Known for its established ecosystem and commercial support options, Terraform integrates seamlessly with cloud providers and HashiCorp tools like Vault and Consul. 𝗢𝗽𝗲𝗻𝗧𝗼𝗳𝘂: For companies valuing open-source freedom, OpenTofu offers the same IaC capabilities without the usage restrictions of BSL. It&#x27;s ideal for avoiding lock-in and for teams that want control over their infrastructure roadmap. 𝗖𝗼𝘀𝘁-𝗕𝗲𝗻𝗲𝗳𝗶𝘁 𝗔𝗻𝗮𝗹𝘆𝘀𝗶𝘀 𝗧𝗲𝗿𝗿𝗮𝗳𝗼𝗿𝗺: Offers a free version, but larger companies may opt for Terraform Enterprise for advanced compliance and security, which comes at a cost. However, it&#x27;s well-suited for enterprises needing support. 𝗢𝗽𝗲𝗻𝗧𝗼𝗳𝘂: As a free, open-source tool, OpenTofu eliminates licensing fees. However, it relies on community support, which can be a cost-saving but requires self-reliance for complex issues. 𝗙𝗶𝗻𝗮𝗹 𝗧𝗵𝗼𝘂𝗴𝗵𝘁
