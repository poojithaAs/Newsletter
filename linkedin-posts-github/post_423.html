#Day58: GitLab CI/CD Error Handling – Troubleshooting Deployment Issues Today, we&#x27;re shifting our focus to GitLab CI/CD, another powerful tool for automating your pipelines. Let&#x27;s dive into common GitLab CI/CD errors, explore #whatitmeans, #howithappens, and provide practical #solutions! ⚙️ 🔄 #JobStuck #WhatItMeans: Your job is stuck in the "pending" or "running" state without progressing.

#HowItHappens: No available or misconfigured runners. #Solution: Check the runner status and ensure active runners are assigned under Settings > CI/CD > Runners. 🔑 #JobFailsDueToMissingDependencies #WhatItMeans: Your job fails due to missing dependencies in the environment. #HowItHappens: Required packages or tools are not installed on the runner.

#Solution: Include necessary dependencies in your .gitlab-ci.yml file: yaml before_script: - apt-get update && apt-get install -y <dependency-name> 🚫 #PermissionIssuesWithRunner #WhatItMeans: The runner lacks the necessary permissions to execute tasks. #HowItHappens: File or directory access issues or insufficient Docker permissions.

#Solution: Ensure correct permissions or run the Docker container with proper privileges. ⚡ #ScriptTimedOut #WhatItMeans: Your script or job exceeds the time limit and is terminated. #HowItHappens: The job takes too long to execute.
