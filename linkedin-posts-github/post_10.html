#WeBrokeItTogether Episode 03022026: The "Helpful" Browser Extension Employees used a free browser extension that, unbeknownst to them, was collecting their login credentials.

Sara Baqla (Software Engineer): "Why are we getting login attempts from Brazil in the middle of the night? Our team is asleep!"

Poojitha A S (DevOps Engineer): "Those aren&#x27;t brute-force attacks. Those are legitimate login attempts."

Sara Baqla: "Impossible. Our team uses strong passwords."

Poojitha A S: "Strong passwords don&#x27;t matter if you give them out."

Sara Baqla: "…wait. That coupon extension everyone installed?"

Poojitha A S: "It was collecting saved login credentials and cookies."

Sara Baqla: "So, our server wasn&#x27;t breached."

Poojitha A S: "No, it wasn&#x27;t our server that was breached."

Sara Baqla: "Our browser was!"

Fix: Global password reset Termination of active sessions Prohibition of unauthorized extensions using endpoint policy Implementation of hardware-based MFA

Result: Attack contained in a matter of hours. No data breach confirmed. The largest threat vector in any company today is the browser, not the server. We secured our server... but failed to consider that all users use their personal devices to connect to work, each of which runs 17 extensions.

Future Protection: Zero-trust policies for devices, MFA, and extension allowlisting. #DevOps #SRE #fullStackDev #cybersecurity
