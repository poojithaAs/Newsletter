"Is AIOps replacing DevOps?" Short answer: No. Long answer: Let me tell you a story… What even is AIOps? Think of AIOps (Artificial Intelligence for IT Ops) as your DevOps setup : but now it&#x27;s got

• machine learning,

• big data crunching,

• and pattern-spotting superpowers. It helps with:

• catching incidents before they escalate

• figuring out why things broke (root cause)

• filtering out noise from 500+ alerts

• and yes, giving "predictive insights" that sound cooler than they are But here&#x27;s the talk: AIOps is not a magic wand. It works only when you&#x27;ve already built a solid DevOps foundation. Otherwise? It&#x27;s like putting autopilot in a car with no wheels. You still need:

• Git, CI/CD, Terraform, K8s, monitoring basics

• Strong team culture

• Clean logs (not 400 lines of "it works on my machine") Why it matters: AIOps enhances DevOps. It&#x27;s not here to take over. It helps scale, reduce noise, and speed up action. But without proper pipelines and good hygiene, it has nothing to learn from. Even the big guys agree: Gartner says AIOps is "complementary." AWS and GCP say it&#x27;s part of the observability layer, not a DevOps replacement.

In short: You feed it good DevOps, it gives you smart alerts. So next time someone asks, "How do I start learning AIOps?" Tell them: "Same way you learn AIOps&#x27; older sibling — DevOps. Build the basics, and AIOps will follow." And if they ask for a shortcut… Send them the meme.
