#DAY210 Just wrapped up Co-Intelligence by Ethan Mollick — and I&#x27;ll say this: it&#x27;s not a book, it&#x27;s a mindset upgrade. Shoutout to David Kypuros from RHUG for this gift — it came at the perfect time. I didn&#x27;t read it to "learn AI." I read it to figure out how I can work with AI without losing my DevOps soul. And Mollick gets it. "You are the pilot.

AI is the co." That one line reframed everything I thought I knew. I used to ask ChatGPT questions like I was Googling error logs. Now? I structure prompts like I structure my Terraform modules — clear, layered, reusable, and with enough context to avoid disasters.

If you&#x27;ve read it, you&#x27;ll know the real gems:

• The AI Sandwich: Not just a prompt strategy. It&#x27;s a DevOps debugging ritual now. ("Here&#x27;s the issue. Here&#x27;s the output. Here&#x27;s what I want. Help me, AI.")

• Co-critiquing: I never thought I&#x27;d ask AI to roast my architecture decisions… but here we are.

• Fast, Fluent, Fallible — that line lives rent-free in my brain. It describes both AI and the Jenkins job I inherited last year. What struck me most wasn&#x27;t the power of AI—it was the power of me working with AI. My DevOps mindset isn&#x27;t obsolete. It&#x27;s finally got a power boost — if I use it wisely. This isn&#x27;t about fear. It&#x27;s about co-creation.

And no, AI isn&#x27;t going to replace us anytime soon… But it might help us stop accidentally deleting production resources. (Looking at you, Friday merges.) Thank you again, David. This wasn&#x27;t just a book. It was a mirror, a debugger, and a compass. #CoIntelligence #AIinDevOps
