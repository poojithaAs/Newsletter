#Day140 #ErrorHandlingseries with #SoftSkills – Day 1/5 How I Found the Root Cause… in the Logs No One Read" 3 AM. #PagerDuty alert. The application is randomly crashing every Monday at 2:07 AM. No clear error messages. No alerts. No clue why.

Theories start flying: "It must be Kubernetes!" "Maybe a network issue?" "Could be a cosmic event." Everyone checks their own area—infra, network, database—but finds nothing. Meanwhile, the crashes continue. So I take a different approach. Instead of looking at recent logs, I decide to dig deep into the oldest debug logs.

And then… buried in a forgotten log file: "Scheduled memory cleanup process executed at 2:07 AM." It turns out, a cron job was force-killing long-running processes every Monday, including the app. Lesson? CI/CD will deploy your code, but curiosity will save your system. Sometimes, the root cause isn&#x27;t where everyone is looking—it&#x27;s where no one is looking.

Ever solved a weird bug just by looking where no one else did?
