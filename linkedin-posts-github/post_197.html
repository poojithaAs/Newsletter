#DAY225 Chaos, Curiosity, and Community: My Volunteer Journey at KCD Texas 2025 I didn&#x27;t just attend KCD Texas 2025 :I volunteered, learned, connected, and left inspired. It was a day full of hallway chats, brain-bending sessions, and one giant reminder: The future is open source. And it&#x27;s already here.

Here&#x27;s what blew my mind: 1. "K8s vs Agent Smith" Andrew Martin We&#x27;re giving AI models more power than ever—but are we securing the pipelines behind them?

From prompt injections to LLM jailbreaks, Andrew showed how Kubernetes and AI are deeply intertwined, and why security must evolve with scale. "You can&#x27;t outsource accountability to a machine."

2. Ricardo Rocha Rocha (CERN): 30,000+ GPUs. Petabytes of data. Nobel-level science reproduced in minutes—thanks to Kubernetes. It was humbling to realize how DevOps principles are accelerating discoveries at the edge of physics.

3. Lin Sun GenAI App Demo: She built, deployed, and iterated a full generative AI app—live. It wasn&#x27;t just informative. It was a masterclass in what&#x27;s possible when you combine clarity, code, and community.

4. CI/CD pipelines, Policy-as-Code, and Prometheus nostalgia: From secure deployment diagrams to CNCF&#x27;s 10-year reflection, I left with actionable insights and a serious to-do list for improving the systems I work on.

5. The people. Always the people. I was lucky to volunteer alongside folks like Dr. Damian Igbe meet the amazing Sammy Cheung, and work with a phenomenal team.
