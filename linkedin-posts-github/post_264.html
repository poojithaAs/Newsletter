#DAY176 Understanding Cloud Storage Tiers Cloud storage isn&#x27;t a one-size-fits-all solution. It comes in different tiers designed for different needs. Storing everything in hot storage might seem like the easiest option, but it&#x27;s also the most expensive. Choosing the right storage tier can help balance cost and performance. What is Cloud Storage Tiering?

Cloud providers like AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud offer multiple storage tiers based on how often data is accessed and how quickly it needs to be retrieved. #HotStorage (Most expensive) Used for frequently accessed data that requires fast performance. Ideal for active databases, real-time analytics, and frequently used files.

#WarmStorage (Moderate cost) Designed for data that is accessed occasionally, such as a few times per month. Works well for backups, logs, and periodically accessed data. #ColdStorage (Lower cost) Best for data that is rarely accessed, such as compliance storage and disaster recovery. Storage is cheap, but retrieval is slower and can be more expensive.

#ArchiveStorage (Cheapest option) Meant for data that is almost never accessed, like legal records and historical backups. Extremely low storage cost, but retrieval can take hours or days. Why Does This Matter? Many businesses store all their data in hot storage because it&#x27;s fast and always available. But in most cases, that&#x27;s not necessary and leads to unnecessarily high costs.
