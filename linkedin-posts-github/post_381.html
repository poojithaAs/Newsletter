#DAY92 #What is OAuth and Why Does it Matter? Ever used the "Sign in with Google" or "Login with Facebook" button? That&#x27;s OAuth in action! #OAuth (Open Authorization) is a security framework that allows apps to access your information without needing your password. Think of it like giving a friend a spare key to just your living room, not your whole house.

Here&#x27;s how it works in three easy steps: You Approve Access: When an app (like a fitness app) asks to access your Google account, OAuth steps in. You&#x27;re redirected to Google to log in, but you never give the app your Google password. Google Issues a Key (Token): After you approve, Google gives a unique "access token" to the app.

This token lets the app access ONLY the data you allowed, like your calendar or contacts. Limited Access, Full Security: Now, the app can fetch data (like your schedule) without storing your credentials. #Why Use OAuth?

Keeps your password private Limits access to only what you approve Provides temporary access, revokable anytime #OAuth is the technology behind seamless logins, secure data sharing, and safe access in the apps we use every day.

Next time you log in with Google or Facebook, you can thank OAuth for keeping things both simple and secure!
