#DAY187 Scaling with Terraform Isn&#x27;t Just About More Resources Most teams think scaling with Terraform means:

• Increasing EC2 instance counts

• Adding more nodes to EKS

• Boosting RDS capacity That&#x27;s horizontal scaling, sure. But real Terraform scaling happens in how you structure your code: Here&#x27;s what smart scaling looks like:

• Modular design – Reusable, composable modules across teams

• Workspaces – Environment-specific state management (dev, stage, prod)

• Remote backends – Scalable state storage and team collaboration

• DRY principles – Less copy-paste, fewer bugs, better Git hygiene

• Terragrunt or Atlantis – Scaling governance and workflows without chaos Pro tip: If your Terraform repo is 5,000 lines in a single main.tf— you didn&#x27;t scale your infra, you just scaled your tech debt. CTA: Want to scale infra without losing sanity? It starts with scalable code. #Terraform #DevOps #InfrastructureAsCode
