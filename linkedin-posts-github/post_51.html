AI Got Better When We Stopped Treating It Like Magic I attended "Beyond Vibes: Converting AI Hype into a Trust Dividend" at Improving led by Tim Rayburn. The most useful part wasn&#x27;t a model comparison. It was a process shift. Tim walked through a framework called #BMAD not as a tool that "writes code," but as a way to:

• Get ideas out of our heads and onto disk

• Preserve decisions so AI doesn&#x27;t forget them

• Stop AI from rewriting everything when only one thing changed

• Treat AI like a teammate that follows rules, not a chatbot guessing intent A simple but powerful distinction: Chatbots talk. Agents act. #BMAD helps AI:

• Take notes

• Read and write files

• Respect constraints

• Carry context across work, not just prompts AI doesn&#x27;t struggle because it&#x27;s dumb. It struggles because we don&#x27;t give it durable context. Thanks Tim Rayburn for a very grounded, practical session.
