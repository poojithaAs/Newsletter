#DAY217 Can&#x27;t afford certifications? You&#x27;re not alone. I couldn&#x27;t either. But that didn&#x27;t stop me from learning DevOps, Terraform, Kubernetes, and cloud. Here&#x27;s what I did—and what you can do too:

•

1. Download the exam syllabus Use it as your learning guide. No need to guess what to study.

•

2. Watch free YouTube content There are incredible creators breaking down AWS, Terraform, and more—for free.

•

3. Practice with mock exams Even failing mock tests taught me more than any theory ever could.

•

4. Build real stuff Set up a pipeline. Deploy an app. Create a Terraform module. Break things. Fix them. Repeat.

•

5. Read the docs (yes, really) They&#x27;re not as boring as they sound—and they teach you how things actually work.

•

6. Share your journey online Your progress is your portfolio. Documenting helps others, too. Certifications are helpful—but skills are what get you hired. If you can&#x27;t spend money, spend time. It&#x27;s just as valuable—maybe even more.
