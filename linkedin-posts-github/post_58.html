#EPISODE 1 : THE 3 AM DISASTER The Cron Job That Killed Checkout

Sara Baqla: "So… I wrote a cleanup script."

Poojitha A S: "Cool. What&#x27;s it clean?"

Sara Baqla: "Old orders. Runs at 3 AM every day."

Poojitha A S: "Did you add an index on the date column?"

Sara Baqla: "Index? Does it really matter?"

Poojitha A S: "Did you test it on prod-sized data?"

Sara Baqla: "It worked fine on my 50 test orders!"

Poojitha A S: "How many orders are in prod?"

Sara Baqla: "…4.7 million?"

Poojitha A S: "Sara."

Sara Baqla: "Yeah?"

Poojitha A S: "We&#x27;re screwed." WHAT ACTUALLY #HAPPENED 3:00 AM — Cron job starts 3:02 AM — Database locks up 3:05 AM — Users can&#x27;t checkout 3:47 AM — Alerts finally wake us up 4:35 AM — Job killed manually #Damage: 2,847 failed checkouts $18K revenue lost THE

#FIX ✅ Sara added the missing index

• Query time: 12 minutes → 0.3 seconds ✅ Poojitha rescheduled the job to 2 AM (low traffic) ✅ Added a 30-second query timeout ✅ Tested on staging with real data size (this time) #LESSONS LEARNED Sara Baqla learned: "Test with realistic data size." Poojitha A S learned: "Ask about indexes before deployment." Both of us learned: "If Slack pings at 3 AM… answer it or suffer." WHOSE #FAULT WAS IT?

Both of us. And that&#x27;s the point. Sara Baqla wrote code without thinking about scale. Poojitha A S deployed without asking the right questions. We #brokeit together. We #fixedit together.
