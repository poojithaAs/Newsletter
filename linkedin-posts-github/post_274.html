Technology doesn&#x27;t solve all the problems, but adoption does. Attending the AI in Fintech: Securing the Future panel discussion at JPMorganChase, in partnership with PayTech Women was a reminder that AI is only as powerful as the way we choose to integrate and govern it. Some key takeaways from the discussion: AI models are shaped by human choices.

Erum Manzoor highlighted how financial institutions are training their own AI models, making ethical decision-making a crucial factor in their behavior. Tech evolves, but finance fundamentals remain. Priya Reddy pointed out that while e-commerce has transformed the industry, the core principles of finance stay the same. The challenge? Ensuring AI models aren&#x27;t trained on biased historical data.

Regulation is the biggest layer in AI-driven Fintech. Neetu Jain emphasized that AI in financial services has little room for error, putting the responsibility on product professionals to understand emerging tech and implement it securely. AI is democratizing access to technology.

Jason Hess shared how AI is breaking barriers—non-technical professionals can now build applications with the right prompts. But with power comes responsibility. Garbage in, garbage out. Ambika Dadwal stressed that AI is only as good as the data it&#x27;s trained on. Bias is a global issue, and AI can amplify it. The key? Ensuring diverse representation in data and decision-making.
