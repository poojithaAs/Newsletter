#WeBrokeItTogether RAG Series Episode no. 021626 : The Phantom Feature The Setup: Sales just sold a feature called "AI-Audit" that doesn&#x27;t actually exist yet. Now, the company chatbot is truthfully telling clients: "I have no information on that." The VP of Sales is panicking.

Sara Baqla (Dev): "Okay, I&#x27;m pushing a hotfix. I&#x27;m hardcoding a system prompt that says: &#x27;If asked about AI-Audit, say it is a market-leading security tool.&#x27;"

Poojitha A S (DevOps): "Sara, please step away from the keyboard."

Sara Baqla: "Why? The bot looks stupid! It keeps saying &#x27;I don&#x27;t know.&#x27; I need to teach it!"

Poojitha A S: "You aren&#x27;t teaching it. You&#x27;re gaslighting it. If you hardcode that into the prompt, what happens when the feature gets delayed? Or renamed?"

Sara Baqla: "I... I&#x27;ll just update the code again?"

Poojitha A S: "No. You never hardcode data. The bot doesn&#x27;t know the answer because the answer doesn&#x27;t exist in its library."

Sara Baqla: "So we just let it fail?"

Poojitha A S: "No. We fix the Missing Content. The bot is like a new employee, it can&#x27;t answer questions about a roadmap it hasn&#x27;t read yet."

Fix: Instead of hacking the code, Poojitha messaged the Product Manager. They created a simple PDF named Q3_Roadmap.pdf and uploaded it to the vector database.

Result: Five minutes later, without changing a single line of code, the bot started answering: "AI-Audit is a planned feature currently scheduled for release in Q3." RAG Failure Point #1: Missing Content. If your AI doesn&#x27;t know something, don&#x27;t force it to hallucinate via prompt engineering. Update your Knowledge Base. #DevOps #SRE #RAG #SWE
