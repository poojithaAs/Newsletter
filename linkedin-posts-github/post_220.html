#DAY207 The Most Overlooked DevOps Skill: Actually listening. (And no, nodding while thinking about lunch doesn&#x27;t count.) In tech, we love showing off:

• How many tools we can name in one breath,

• How many certs are hanging on the wall,

• How many frameworks we can flex in meetings like buzzwords. But honestly? The real flex isn&#x27;t knowing more ; It&#x27;s listening better. Listening to:

• What users are actually struggling with (not just their wishlist).

• What dashboards are whispering before they start screaming.

• What teammates are hinting at when they say, "hmm… it&#x27;s kinda slow."

• What clusters and pipelines are silently crying about before they rage-quit. Silent signals > Loud firefighting. Always. The best engineers I&#x27;ve met? They&#x27;re not the ones talking the most. They&#x27;re the ones picking up tiny signals everyone else misses and fixing things before it becomes a 3 AM disaster.

Kinda like being a good husband, too. (Pretty sure Sravan Dasari totally agree with me on this one!) Because whether it&#x27;s production systems or relationships: Ignore the small signs, and you&#x27;re signing up for trouble. Listen early. Fix faster. Sleep better. #DevOps #LifeSkills #SoftSkillsMatter
