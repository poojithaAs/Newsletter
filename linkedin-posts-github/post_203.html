#DAY221 No one is truly safe. And that&#x27;s the scariest part. I&#x27;ve had four very different conversations this week, but they all felt the same. A fresher in tears after dozens of rejections.

A mid-level engineer constantly worried, asking, "What if I&#x27;m next?" Me, with nine years of experience, still battling that voice: "Am I doing enough?" And the hardest one.a senior leader with 25 years in the industry. They spent the last seven years leading teams, mentoring others, showing up every day with loyalty and heart. Still, they were let go.

They told me, "I never imagined this would happen to me. I gave it everything." I didn&#x27;t know what to say. Just sat in silence. Because the truth is,this could happen to any of us. Not because we aren&#x27;t good enough. Not because we didn&#x27;t work hard. But because the world is shifting faster than ever. The only real protection now is adaptability. Not another course. Not the perfect resume.

Not even years of service. But the willingness to start over. To stay curious. To learn something new even when you don&#x27;t want to. That&#x27;s what keeps us moving when everything around us feels stuck. We&#x27;re all sitting in the fire. Some just starting. Some after decades. But one thing helps us breathe through it: "This is fine.if I&#x27;ve got adaptability".
