#Episode3 THE INFINITE RETRY LOOP (HOW WE ACCIDENTALLY ATTACKED OURSELVES) #DDOS = Overwhelmed with too many requests at once #WeBrokeItTogether

Sara Baqla (#FullStackDeveloper): "I deployed a tiny change."

Poojitha A S (#DevOps / #SRE): "Cool. Why is traffic exploding?"

Sara Baqla: "There&#x27;s no user spike."

Poojitha A S: "Then it&#x27;s internal."

Sara Baqla: "…Retries?"

Poojitha A S: "Yep. How many?"

Sara Baqla: "Three per request."

Poojitha A S: "And how many failed together?"

Sara Baqla: "…Thousands."

Poojitha A S: "Okay. That explains it." #BOTH: "Let&#x27;s fix it together." WHAT #HAPPENED

• Downstream slowed

• Thousands of requests failed

• Each retried (within limits)

• Retries fired together

• Traffic multiplied

• Alerts everywhere Imagine there&#x27;s only one restaurant open on Christmas Eve, and you need to eat out because your home-cooked turkey was cooked by Clark Griswold. So they call the restaurant to check. Each customer is polite and only calls up to 3 times. But there&#x27;s no coordination between them. Some call now. Some call again a few seconds later. Some hang up and retry. The #problem?

The phone lines never clear. THE #ISSUE Retry limits existed. They were per request, not per system. Safe locally. Dangerous globally. THE

#FIX Retry jitter: Random delays prevent thundering herd Circuit breakers: Stop retries when unhealthy System-wide retry budget: Global coordination Retry storm alerts: Detect early THE

#LESSON Retries aren&#x27;t bad. Uncoordinated retries are. Not a #Dev issue. Not a #DevOps issue. A #system issue. #NEXT The Alert That Fired 9,000 Times #DevOps #SRE #ProductionStories #FullstackDeveloper #Devloper #EngineeringLife
