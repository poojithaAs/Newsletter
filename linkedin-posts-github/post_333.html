#DAY132 #Labels vs. #Service Selectors in Kubernetes In Kubernetes, everything revolves around YAML manifests. A resource&#x27;s metadata describes the resource itself (like a Pod, Deployment, or Service), while the spec defines how it should behave. Labels in metadata These are key-value tags you attach to an object.

For example: metadata: labels: app: my-app environment: production They help organize, group, and search your resources. You can run kubectl get pods -l app=my-app to find all Pods labeled app=my-app, for instance. #Service Selectors A Service uses spec.selector to find the Pods it routes traffic to.

It looks for Pods whose labels match the selector: spec: selector: app: my-app This means the Service&#x27;s own labels (metadata.labels) are purely for identifying the Service itself. They do not decide which Pods receive traffic. Why This Matters Decoupling how you label the Service from the labels on Pods lets you organize resources differently and maintain flexibility.

#Summary #metadata.labels = "I am a resource with these labels." #Service&#x27;s spec.selector = "I want to find Pods that have these labels." Remember: Services match Pods by spec.selector, not by the Service&#x27;s own labels.
