#Day114 When Exposing Ports in Kubernetes Doesn&#x27;t Work: A Common Frustration" You&#x27;ve set up your Kubernetes service and exposed the port, but when you try to access it—nothing. It&#x27;s one of those classic issues that every DevOps and SRE engineer encounters. Common Reasons for Port Exposure Failures:

1. Incorrect Service Type:

• ClusterIP is the default and doesn&#x27;t expose a port outside the cluster.

• You might need a NodePort or LoadBalancer service depending on your access requirements.

2. Firewall or Security Groups:

• Ports may be blocked by cloud security groups or local firewalls, preventing external access.

3. Incorrect Port Mapping:

• Double-check your spec.ports in the service YAML to ensure the ports are correctly defined.

• Ensure that the pod is indeed listening on the expected port.

4. Pod Networking:

• Networking issues within the cluster, such as pod-to-pod communication failures or issues with the CNI (Container Network Interface).

5. NodePort Range Misconfiguration:

• In some cases, a NodePort service might fall outside the allowed port range (default is 30000–32767). Troubleshooting Tips:

1. Use kubectl get svc: Check your service&#x27;s status, endpoints, and ports.

2. Check Pod Logs: Ensure your app is running properly inside the pod and listening on the right port.

3. Access via Cluster IP: Test internal communication using the ClusterIP before trying to expose externally.
