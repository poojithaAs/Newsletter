Day 2 at Devopsdays Dallas – Deep dives, tough truths, and a lot of AI Some highlights that stuck with me today: Bryan Finster – "AI is Taking Our Jobs!" AI is closing the gap between average and excellent developers — but if our orgs still run on big requirements, silos, fragile releases, manual approvals… then faster devs just mean faster chaos. "Every line of undelivered code is inventory waste and delivery risk." The real foundation is still clear goals, testable requirements, automated pipelines, and modern practices (TDD, CI/CD, value stream thinking). Only then does AI become an accelerator, not a liability. Ignite Talks – rapid-fire lessons

• Docker Bake → cleaner, composable builds with validation baked in.

• Developer Portals → don&#x27;t just build and force adoption. Use design thinking: empathize, define, ideate, prototype, test, then implement.

• Forecast Capital One → compliance like spell-check. Errors/fixes appear inline in VS Code before CI. Meeting devs where they are = game changer. Open Spaces

• otel (OpenTelemetry) → flexibility and vendor independence are big wins, but cost, cardinality, and weak docs are pain points. Tesla&#x27;s federated model with Clickhouse + Prometheus sparked great discussion.

• ML replacing platform engineering? → consensus: AI won&#x27;t replace platform engineers, but it will reshape the role. Guardrails, prompt engineering, and embedding org-specific rules are where value lies. Panel Talk The biggest risks of AI aren&#x27;t technical — they&#x27;re cultural: laziness, hallucinations, and compliance drift.

Platforms must balance guardrails + autonomy: non-negotiables for security/resilience, but extensibility for team-driven quality. "Escalation isn&#x27;t a dirty word. It&#x27;s how we align and learn." My takeaway: Tools evolve fast, but culture and discipline are still the hardest problems in DevOps. AI won&#x27;t take your job — but someone who knows how to pair AI with DevOps discipline just might.

TL;DR

• AI is powerful, but without solid pipelines + culture it amplifies chaos.

• Developer portals & compliance tools must meet devs where they are.

• OpenTelemetry is promising but still rough at scale.

• AI won&#x27;t replace platform engineers, it will reshape them.

• Culture > tools. Always. Sravan Dasari Mani Senthil Vijeta Digarse
