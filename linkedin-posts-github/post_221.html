#DAY206 In DevOps, we love big names: Kubernetes, Terraform, Docker, Jenkins… But real day-to-day survival? It often depends on tools that nobody brags about in presentations. Here&#x27;s my list of the most underrated DevOps tools — and why they matter: 1. tmux / screen Terminal session managers. Imagine you&#x27;re mid-deployment on a remote server… and your SSH session drops.

With tmux, your work stays alive. You reconnect and pick up where you left off. Lifesaver. Especially for production debugging. 2. jq Lightweight JSON processor. You&#x27;ll be swimming in JSON in cloud + DevOps work — API calls, Kubernetes manifests, Terraform outputs. Instead of opening it in an editor, jq lets you filter, parse, format JSON right from the terminal. 3. curl Every API&#x27;s best friend.

Forget Postman. When you need to quickly test an endpoint, check headers, debug a webhook — curl is instant, powerful, and scriptable. 4. netcat (nc) The Swiss Army knife of networking. Is a port open? Can I reach that server? Need to test sending/receiving raw data? Netcat does it. Lightweight and irreplaceable for quick network troubleshooting. 5. htop A better way to see system resources.

Why struggle with top&#x27;s clunky display? htop gives you a clear, color-coded, scrollable view of your system&#x27;s CPU, memory, and processes. Perfect for spotting bottlenecks fast. Truth is: The tools that save your job at 2 AM during an outage… Are rarely the ones shown off in webinars. Master the underrated ones.
