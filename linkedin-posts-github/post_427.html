#Day54 🚀 #ErrorHandlingSeries - #Networking Networking issues can bring down even the most resilient pipelines. Let&#x27;s walk through 4 common networking errors, #whatitmeans, #howithappens, and real-world #solutions! 🌐 🔌 #ConnectionRefused #WhatItMeans: A service is refusing to accept your connection on a specific port.

This often happens when the service is not running or a firewall is blocking the connection. #Scenario: While working on deploying a web application, the service failed to start, showing a "Connection refused" error when I tried to access it. Upon investigation, I found that #Nginx was not running due to a configuration change during a restart.

#HowItWasFixed: I checked the status of the #Nginx service and found it wasn&#x27;t running.

Starting the service manually and verifying the firewall rules resolved the issue. 👉 #Solution: sudo systemctl start nginx to start the service and sudo ufw allow 80 to open the #HTTP port. 🔄 #NetworkTimeout #WhatItMeans: A network request is timing out, indicating a delay or failure in reaching the destination server.

This is often due to slow or congested #network routes, or incorrect #routing configuration. #Scenario: In a #CI/CD pipeline, pulling dependencies from an external server was timing out. A traceroute showed that packets were getting dropped at one of the intermediate hops due to congestion.
