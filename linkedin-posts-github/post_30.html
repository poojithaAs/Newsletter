#WeBrokeItTogether RAG Series Episode no. 021526 RAG promised to solve your AI knowledge problem without the cost of retraining. Your team implemented it but are you actually seeing the ROI? This week&#x27;s episodes break down 7 RAG breakpoints costing companies time, money and trust.

Sara Baqla: "Why is the company chatbot telling me we only get 10 vacation days when I know it&#x27;s 15?"

Poojitha A S: "Because it was trained on old docs from 2 years ago. This is actually the perfect scenario to implement Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG)! Instead of training the model on absolutely everything, it retrieves the info needed only when asked."

Sara Baqla: "How does it do that exactly?"

Poojitha A S: "Well, LLMs have powerful general purpose capabilities, but they know nothing about your private content like your wikis, tickets, emails, or databases. RAG solves this by converting your question into an embedding and searching for documents with similar embeddings.

When you ask about vacation days, it retrieves the current HR policy docs and feeds them to the model at query time."

Sara Baqla: "So it always has the latest info? And we don&#x27;t have to retrain the model every time HR updates a policy?"

Poojitha A S: "Exactly! Just update the docs and RAG automatically picks up the new info. Way more cost effective than retraining, and it keeps responses current without any downtime."

Sara Baqla: "Sounds like a perfect solution! So RAG can&#x27;t break, right?"

Poojitha A S: "Ha! You have to consider bad chunking, embeddings mismatch, retrieval failures, context window limits... among other things. You should really check out the Developer&#x27;s Guide to AI by Danny Thompson, Jerry Mannel Reghunadh and Jacob Orshalick for a deep dive." #SRE #devOps #fullStackDeveloper
