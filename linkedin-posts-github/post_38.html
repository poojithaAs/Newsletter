#WeBrokeItTogether This Week in #Kubernetes #Autoscaling (6 parts) #Part2/6: Why 100+ Clusters Become Unmanageable Airbnb had 100+ Kubernetes clusters. Every upgrade took months.

Sara Baqla: "Why did Airbnb have 100+ clusters?"

Poojitha A S: "Different workloads need different machines. GPU. High-memory. CPU-optimized."

Sara Baqla: "So you separate them?"

Poojitha A S: "Until you need to upgrade. Then you&#x27;re testing 30+ cluster types."

Sara Baqla: "That sounds... tedious."

Poojitha A S: "30+ configurations. 30+ rollout plans. Months of meetings about meetings."

Sara Baqla: "What if something breaks?"

Poojitha A S: "You&#x27;re debugging across 100+ clusters. It&#x27;s like finding a typo in a 100-page document."

Sara Baqla: "So more clusters = more chaos?"

Poojitha A S: "Exponentially. It&#x27;s like owning 100 cars instead of

5. Sure, you have options. But now you&#x27;re spending all your time at the mechanic."

The #

Fix: Went from 100+ specialized clusters to ~40 smart clusters.

The #Lesson: More clusters = more operational headaches. Not better isolation. #Next: How they ran GPU, Intel, AMD, and high-memory workloads in ONE cluster (and didn&#x27;t lose their minds).
