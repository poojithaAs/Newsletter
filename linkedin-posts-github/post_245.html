We all know how important security is—but are we truly building cyber resilience at the leadership level? The Official Cybersecurity Summit delivered just that—a reality check on where we stand and where we must go.

From insightful keynotes to real-world strategies, the event was packed with powerful takeaways—and meaningful conversations on how cyber resilience needs to be a shared responsibility across IT, DevOps, and the C-suite. One of the key highlights? A deep dive discussion around AI, types of data, classification, and securing sensitive information through policy enforcement.

Cybersecurity is no longer a siloed function—it&#x27;s a strategic, cross-functional priority. Jayaprakash (JP) Cheenepalli Ala Shwayat, P.E. Here&#x27;s what stood out:

• Legacy systems lack context—making data hard to protect and prioritize.

• Data is everywhere: structured, unstructured, and growing exponentially.

• The insider threat is real—adopting an "assume breach" posture is no longer optional.

• Automation, awareness, and risk-based thinking are the future of cybersecurity. Key next steps for organizations:

1. Integrate smarter data classification frameworks into every workflow.

2. Drive cross-functional collaboration on AI governance and compliance.

3. Embed security automation into CI/CD pipelines and audit cycles.

4. Create space for continuous learning and intelligence sharing. A huge thank you to the sponsors and partners who made this event possible: Horizon3.ai LevelBlue
