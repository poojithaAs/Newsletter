#WeBrokeItTogether : This Week in Kubernetes Autoscaling (6 parts) Sara Baqla (#FullstackDeveloper) Poojitha A S (#Devops #SRE) This week we&#x27;re tackling the most misunderstood Kubernetes topic: Autoscaling. Most teams think &#x27;HPA is enabled, we&#x27;re good.&#x27; Then the bill shows up. And it&#x27;s somehow... worse.

We&#x27;re taking one real-world scale story (Airbnb-style problems) and turning it into six tiny lessons you can actually remember. Or at least laugh about. Here&#x27;s what&#x27;s coming: #Day1: Pods vs Nodes HPA adds pods. But it can&#x27;t create the machines those pods need. So you get pending pods. And a bigger bill. And regret. #Day2: The 100+ Cluster Trap Separating everything feels clean.

Until upgrades, monitoring, and incidents become your full-time job. Welcome to cluster hell. #Day3: One Cluster, Many Node Types GPU + AMD + Intel + high-memory in one place sounds chaotic. It is. But Kubernetes actually knows what it&#x27;s doing (sometimes). #Day4: The Autoscaler "Guessing Game" Scaling is easy.

Picking the right node type is where the cost, capacity, and performance battles start. And where you lose sleep. #Day5: The Smarter Decision Layer When your scale is big enough, the autoscaler needs to ask your business logic what to do. Because the default logic is basically a coin flip.

#Day6:The Reliability Cleanup Early abort + caching: the unsexy optimizations that stop retries, throttling, and wasted minutes. Boring. Effective. Beautiful. #Devops #SRE #FullstackDevleoper
