#WeBrokeItTogether: Reliability for AI Episode 022826: The Cash Registers Went Silent, and Nobody Could Fix It for 16 Hours

The #Setup Walmart. The biggest retailer on earth. $11.8 billion spent on tech. In January 2026, cash registers stopped working across thousands of stores. Customers couldn&#x27;t pay. It took 16.5 hours to fix.

Sara Baqla (Dev): "How does Walmart lose cash registers for 16 hours?"

Poojitha A S (DevOps/SRE): "Network switches went unmanaged. The devices routing data between stores and systems just stopped. Registers had nowhere to send transactions. So they froze."

Sara Baqla: "They have an AI observability platform. Nobody caught this?"

Poojitha A S: "Monitoring was watching apps and AI models. The network switches underneath failed silently. No alert. No log. For 16.5 hours Walmart wasn&#x27;t in control of its own network. Customer payment data flowing through uncontrolled pipes."

Sara Baqla: "One-time thing?"

Poojitha A S: "Leaked emails showed 16 major incidents in eight weeks. Two per week. This one took 16.5 hours because it was infrastructure, not an application. And nobody was watching infrastructure."

The #Fix Monitor network switches as seriously as applications. Alert on unmanaged states before they cascade. Build a failover so one switch failure doesn&#x27;t kill registers in thousands of stores.
