#WeBrokeItTogether: Reliability for AI Episode 022726: The GPU That Passed Every Test and Lied Every Time

The #Setup A company is training an AI model across 16,384 GPUs. Weeks in, millions spent, and the model isn&#x27;t learning properly. No crashes. No errors. Dashboards say everything is healthy. The dev team spends three days tearing through code. Nothing wrong.

Sara Baqla (Dev): "Code is clean. Data is clean. Model design is solid. But the output is garbage."

Poojitha A S (SRE): "Have you looked at the hardware?"

Sara Baqla: "Dashboards say all GPUs are healthy."

Poojitha A S: "Dashboards check if a GPU is alive. They don&#x27;t check if it&#x27;s doing math correctly. It&#x27;s called silent data corruption. The chip doesn&#x27;t crash or throw errors. It just quietly gives you 2+2=5 and acts like nothing happened."

Sara Baqla: "How often does that happen?"

Poojitha A S: "Meta found 6 silent corruption events in 54 days across 16,384 GPUs. Google sees it every week or two. And the nasty part is one chip doing bad math doesn&#x27;t look like a hardware problem. It looks like a bad model. That&#x27;s why your team spent three days blaming the code."

Sara Baqla: "So how do you catch a chip that&#x27;s lying?"

Poojitha A S: "You check its homework. Run the same calculation on two machines and compare. Watch the loss curve for sudden jumps. Look for NaN values. The chip will never tell you it&#x27;s broken. You have to prove it."
