Empathy: The Ultimate Measure of Success Success isn&#x27;t just about how far you go—it&#x27;s about how many people you lift along the way. I once met someone who had nothing. Born into poverty, with every odd stacked against them, they built an empire through sheer resilience. But what made them truly inspiring wasn&#x27;t just their success—it was their kindness.

They mentored others, gave back, and stayed humble, using their journey to uplift those still struggling. On the other hand, I&#x27;ve met people who also worked relentlessly to succeed but carried a different mindset. They believed success was only about their hard work, dismissing others as just "lucky" if they didn&#x27;t struggle the same way.

Their achievements made them feel superior, forgetting that every journey is different. The truth? No one makes it alone. Even if no one helped you, be the person who helps others. The greatest leaders aren&#x27;t just the most successful—they&#x27;re the most empathetic. Be a mentor. Or find one. And always choose kindness.

That&#x27;s the real legacy.
