I had the chance to moderate our #KubeCon2025 panel — thank you Damian Igbe, PhD for organizing and Improving for hosting such a great community session. A huge thank you to our panelists: Danny Williams, Napoleon Musonera, Jeff Holsinger, and Jeremy Hallock for sharing insights. Here are the 10 takeaways the audience walked away with: 1 AI is moving fast, but it needs guardrails.

Kubernetes can run AI agents, but only with safe protocols like A2A and MCP. 2 Image security is the biggest real risk. Too many teams still pull public images without checking anything. 3 Dashboards aren&#x27;t the answer. The real shift is toward AI-assisted SRE that reduces noise and speeds up troubleshooting. 4 Not everything should be a microservice.

One VM can sometimes be safer, simpler, and easier to manage. 5 Supply chain security needs more attention. Version tags can be misleading. Signed images and SHAs matter more than ever. 6 AI will split engineering roles. You&#x27;ll either build AI or learn to use it extremely well — the "middle" work is shrinking. 7 Kubernetes will get both simpler and more complex.

Simpler for people using AI tools, more complex for people building AI systems. 8 Most learning at KubeCon came from real conversations. Hallways, maintainers, demo theaters — not the slides. 9 Tools worth watching: NewVector, Chainguard Images, Kubescape, Feast, Kubeflow, Argo, OTel improvements. 10 Final reminder from the panel: Secure early. Learn deeply. Talk to people.

Community teaches more than keynotes. Grateful to all our panelists and everyone who joined, asked questions, and stayed to network.
