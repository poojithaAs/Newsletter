#WeBrokeItTogether RAG Series Episode 022226 : The Missing Step The Setup: The team uses the bot to generate "Release Notes" from Jira tickets. The bot successfully lists 4 out of 5 bug fixes but skips the most critical security patch. The security team is not amused.

Sara Baqla (Dev): "It&#x27;s skipping data! The context window is big enough. The data is there. The LLM is just lazy!"

Poojitha A S (DevOps): "What was your prompt?" @

Sara: "Summarize the tickets in this sprint."

Poojitha A S: "Define &#x27;Summarize&#x27;."

Sara Baqla: "You know... tell me what happened."

Poojitha A S: "To an LLM, &#x27;Summarize&#x27; means &#x27;Compress&#x27;. It looked at 5 tickets, decided 4 were enough to get the gist, and dropped the last one to save space. It did exactly what you asked."

Sara Baqla: "But I need everything."

Poojitha A S: "Then don&#x27;t ask for a summary."

Fix: Poojitha changed the prompt from "Summarize these tickets" to "List every single ticket ID and title found in the context. Do not omit any items."

Result: The bot returned 5 out of 5 tickets. RAG Failure Point #7: Incomplete. If the LLM is missing pieces of information, it&#x27;s often because your instructions implied that it should. Reword the question to be explicit about completeness. #DevOps #SRE #RAG #SWE #VectorDatabase
