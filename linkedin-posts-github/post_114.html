Day 1 at #DevOpsDaysDallas – Learning, Volunteering & Open Space Day 1 was nothing short of inspiring—packed with deep technical sessions, hallway conversations, and open discussions that reminded me why DevOpsDays is such a unique community event.

I had the privilege of volunteering alongside Mani Senthil and Vijeta Digarse , and I want to sincerely thank the amazing organizers who made this possible: Paula Lipnick, MBA, PMP, PSM Jessica Clark | Roger Servey Scott Purgerson James L.

Baldwin Jack Teoh Travis Mims Russell B. | Mark Andersen John-Paul Herold Gokul Sridharan Highlights from Day 1 talks:

• John Willis – When AI Agents Go Rogue (lessons from polymorphic AI & security)

• Vasu Andolu – Bread&#x27;s Transformation: Innovating with Quality & Strategy

• Ishu Gupta & Sandy Sen – Converging 100s of pipelines into one unified pipeline

• Jeff L. & Neer Patel – Build, Buy, or Ask the Bots? Pricing software in the vibe-coding era

• Pamela Dukes – Getting Better Together: Winning the Corporate Relay

• Kyle Shelton – Building an Observability Center of Excellence

• Seth Eliot – Disaster Recovery strategies for cloud workloads Open Space: AI in DevOps & Operations This was my favorite part of the day. The community-driven conversation touched on trust, adoption, compliance, and how AI is truly reshaping DevOps.

A special recognition to Kalpana Diwakaran Senior Vice President at Ascendion for her powerful contributions:

• How her teams are using AI in platform operations to move from L1 incident elimination → L2 RCA automation with humans in the loop.

• Real-world case study: modernizing investment banking mainframes, cutting understanding + transformation timelines from 6 months down to 6 weeks.

• Driving 45% productivity gains by putting guardrails, tested models, and adoption frameworks in place.

• Emphasizing that AI adoption is not instant—it requires time, psychology, compliance, and structured guardrails.

• Clarifying the difference between automation vs AI—sometimes the right answer is to automate before adding "intelligence." Her insights grounded the room in reality: AI is powerful, but trust, compliance, and human-in-the-loop are essential to scale it responsibly. Feeling grateful for the opportunity to both learn and serve this community. Excited to see what Day 2 brings!
