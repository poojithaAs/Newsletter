#Day25 #Istio vs #Ingress: Key Differences, Benefits, and Architecture 🚀 When managing traffic in #Kubernetes, both Ingress and Istio are powerful tools, but they serve different purposes. Let&#x27;s explore their #differences, #benefits, and #architectures! #Ingress: Simple External Traffic Management 🔑 #Architecture:

• Ingress manages #externaltraffic entering a Kubernetes cluster using #IngressControllers (like NGINX or Traefik) to route traffic.

• Operates at #Layer7 (HTTP/HTTPS) for basic #loadbalancing and #SSL termination. 💡 #KeyBenefits:

• #SimpleRouting: Path-based routing for external requests.

• #Lightweight: Easy setup with minimal overhead.

• #CloudFriendly: Works with cloud-native ingress controllers like AWS ALB.

• #SSLTermination: Manages SSL traffic efficiently. 🚦 #BestUseCase: Ideal for teams needing basic routing and SSL termination for external traffic with minimal setup. #Istio: Advanced Service Mesh Control 🔑 #Architecture:

• Istio is a #ServiceMesh that manages both #internal and #external traffic. It uses #Envoy sidecar proxies for service-to-service communication. 💡 #KeyBenefits:

• #AdvancedTrafficControl: Circuit breaking, fault injection, traffic mirroring.

• #Security: End-to-end encryption using mTLS.

• #Observability: Detailed monitoring with #Prometheus and #Grafana.
