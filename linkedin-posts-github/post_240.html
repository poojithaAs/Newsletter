#DAY192 AI Series: Before ChatGPT — The Rise of Predictive AI Before ChatGPT became a household name… Before LLMs started writing code, poetry, and business plans… AI was already quietly shaping our lives. Let me take you back to where it all began. The Origins: 1950s–1980s AI&#x27;s story began not with algorithms, but with imagination.

Visionaries like Alan Turing and John McCarthy laid the philosophical and mathematical groundwork. Early predictive models were primitive—simple rules and linear regressions on punch cards. Computers weren&#x27;t "smart," but they could calculate trends—basic sales forecasts, stock predictions, weather modeling. The First Shift: 1990s–2000s As data grew, so did our hunger to make sense of it.

Businesses started asking: "Can we predict what customers will do next?" Enter CRM systems, Excel modeling, and early machine learning. Retail, finance, and healthcare were the first to tap in—predicting demand, defaults, and diseases. Prediction became profit. The Explosion: 2010s Big Data. Cloud Computing. Open-source ML libraries. Suddenly, AI was democratized.

Netflix began predicting what you&#x27;d watch. Amazon forecasted your next purchase. Smartphones tracked your habits and suggested what to do next. We weren&#x27;t just analyzing the past—we were shaping the future with code.

Where We Are Now: 2020s Predictive AI is no longer a back-office tool—it&#x27;s a daily life partner.
