#Day104 9 Steps to Master Kubernetes High Availability! High availability (HA) in Kubernetes isn&#x27;t just a buzzword—it&#x27;s a necessity. Here&#x27;s a quick roadmap to get you started:

1. Understand Kubernetes Architecture: Master the control plane and node roles.

2. Set Up Multi-Master Clusters: Ensure no single point of failure.

3. Use Load Balancers: Keep your traffic flowing, even under stress.

4. Leverage Distributed Storage: Ensure data resilience with tools like Ceph or Longhorn.

5. Plan for Disaster Recovery: Backup everything—etcd snapshots are your best friend.

6. Integrate Observability Tools: Monitor, log, and alert with Prometheus, Grafana, or Loki.

7. Harden Security: Use Pod Security Policies and Network Policies to reduce risks.

8. Autoscale Smartly: Horizontal Pod Autoscaler (HPA) isn&#x27;t optional anymore.

9. Test and Improve: Regularly simulate failures to ensure your HA setup actually works. 3 Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. Neglecting Node Availability Zones: Always spread nodes across zones for redundancy.

2. Ignoring Resource Quotas: Over-provisioning can bring your cluster down.

3. Skipping Regular Updates: Outdated clusters are vulnerable clusters.
